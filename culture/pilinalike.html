Pili Nalike

The iconic tiger dance of Tulunadu, performed with roaring drums, bold body paint and electrifying energy during festivals.

What is Pili Nalike?

Pili Nalike (Tiger Dance) is one of the most recognisable folk dance traditions of Tulunadu. Performers paint their entire body with tiger stripes, wear masks or facial makeup, and dance in sync with loud drums in streets and temple surroundings.

The dance is especially popular during Navaratri, Dasara and local temple fairs in Mangaluru, Udupi and surrounding regions. It symbolizes power, courage and celebration.

Origins & Significance

Pili Nalike is deeply connected to the agrarian and temple culture of Tulunadu. Traditionally, teams of performers would visit homes, shops and temples, dancing in return for offerings. The tiger, considered a powerful and divine creature, is invoked to bring protection, strength and prosperity.

Over time, Pili Nalike has grown from a purely ritual performance into a major cultural symbol of coastal Karnataka, while still retaining its spiritual and community roots.

Elements of Pili Nalike

Body Painting

Performers spend hours getting their bodies painted in yellow, orange and black tiger stripes using oil paints or natural dyes. The artwork itself is a form of live folk art.

Drums & Rhythm

The dance is powered by loud chende and drum beats. The rhythm starts slow and gradually builds into high-energy sequences that excite the crowd.

Street & Temple Performances

Groups perform in lanes, markets and temple premises, interacting with people, blessing children and creating a vibrant festive atmosphere throughout the night.
