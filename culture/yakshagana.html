Yakshagana

The Vibrant Theatre Tradition of Tulunadu

What is Yakshagana?

Yakshagana is a centuries-old dance–drama native to Tulunadu. It blends storytelling, music, dance, elaborate costumes and expressive dialogue.

Performances last through the night, narrating stories from Ramayana, Mahabharata and local legends. The rhythmic beats of Chande and Maddale set the stage for powerful performances.

Styles of Yakshagana

Badagu Thittu

  • Popular in Udupi & Kundapura
  • Heavy crown costumes
  • Loud, dynamic drumbeats
  • Colourful makeup

Tenku Thittu

  • Popular in Mangaluru region
  • Natural makeup style
  • More flexible & dance-heavy
  • Known for comedy and dialogue

Costumes

Yakshagana costumes are flamboyant and elaborate. Performers wear headgear, face-paint, ornaments and vibrant garments that reflect mythological characters.

Music

Music is driven by the Bhagavatha (lead singer), accompanied by rhythmic beats of Chande and Maddale.
