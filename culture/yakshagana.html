Yakshagana

The Vibrant Theatre Tradition of Tulunadu

What is Yakshagana?

Yakshagana is a centuries-old dance–drama native to Tulunadu. It blends storytelling, music, dance, elaborate costumes and expressive dialogue.

Performances last through the night, narrating stories from the Ramayana, Mahabharata and local legends. The rhythmic beats of Chande and Maddale create a powerful atmosphere.

Styles of Yakshagana

Badagu Thittu

  • Popular in Udupi & Kundapura
  • Heavy crown costumes
  • Dynamic drumbeats
  • Colourful makeup

Tenku Thittu

  • Popular in Mangaluru region
  • Natural makeup style
  • More flexible & dance-heavy
  • Strong emphasis on dialogue & comedy

Costumes

Yakshagana costumes are flamboyant and elaborate. Performers wear grand headgear, vibrant makeup, ornaments and striking garments that reflect iconic mythological characters.

Music

Music is led by the Bhagavatha (lead singer), supported by the rhythmic beats of Chande and Maddale, creating the heartbeat of every performance.
